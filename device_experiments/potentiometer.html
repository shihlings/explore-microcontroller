Potentiometer

Introduction

The Potentiometer used in this test is a B10K Potentiometer. It can be an analog device or a variable resistor. When the third pin is connected to the Raspberry Pi Pico through Analog-Digital Converter (ADC) ports, the voltage data can be read by the Raspberry Pi Pico. When it is only connected to its two ends, it acts as a variable resistor.

In this test, the program is designed to measure the voltage coming out of the Potentiometer. The Potentiometer can be used to adjust the brightness of the LED Lights and much more.

Demonstration

The program is initiated by the Raspberry Pi 4B (the touch screen in the upper part of the screen), and the Raspberry Pi Pico runs the program.

The program initiates on the Raspberry Pi 4B and passes voltage through the Potentiometer. Then, it continuously prints the voltage detected on the shell script.
